Introduction

This guide explains the complete method, performance modes, and hidden features for the ATMA web application. Please review this document carefully to ensure a smooth and effective performance.

Performance Modes

ATMA features two distinct operational modes, selectable from the settings menu. Understanding the difference is crucial for your performance.

1. Digital QR Mode (Default)

This is a self-contained method. The app collects the spectator's choices and encodes them into a unique URL. This URL is then used to generate a QR code on the final slide of the app. The trick's data is transmitted through the QR code itself. This mode does not require an active internet connection during the performance.

2. Printed QR Mode

This method turns the app into a remote control. It is designed for situations where you want to use a pre-printed QR code (e.g., on a business card). In this mode, the app sends the spectator's choices to a secure database in real-time. The printed QR code links to a webpage that reads this data to display the revelation. This mode requires an active internet connection.

The Settings Menu

To access the settings menu, simply tap the top-right corner of the screen at any time. Inside, you will find the following options:

  • Mode Selection: Switch between Digital QR and Printed QR modes.
  • Custom ID (Printed QR Mode only):
    • Set a unique, secret 6-digit number. This ID links your app to the database, creating your personal "channel".
    • Download QR: Once an ID is saved, this button generates a QR code linked to your channel, ready to be printed.
    • Delete ID: This button permanently removes the currently entered ID and its data from the database. Use with caution.
  • Language Selection: Choose between English and Indonesian for the revelation article.

The Input Method

Regardless of the mode, ATMA uses a consistent gesture-based system to covertly register choices. The method relies on the STARTING AREA of a single SWIPE LEFT gesture.

The screen is divided into five invisible zones. Where you begin your swipe determines the selection.

Performance Flow

For Digital QR Mode:

  1. Introduce the Premise: Explain the experiment. Present the first list of choices (Victim).
  2. Input Choices: For each of the three lists (Victim, Location, Weapon), perform a single SWIPE LEFT from the zone corresponding to the spectator's choice. The app will advance automatically after each swipe.
  3. QR Code Reveal: After the third input, the app displays the final, dynamically generated QR code. Instruct the spectator to scan it.
  4. The Revelation: The spectator's phone opens the news article containing their freely chosen details.

For Printed QR Mode:

  1. Pre-Show Setup: Before the performance, go to Settings, select Printed QR mode, set your 6-digit ID, and use the Download QR button. Print this QR code on a business card or other object.
  2. Introduce the Premise: Hand the printed QR code to the spectator and instruct them to scan it. Their phone will open the prediction page.
  3. Input Choices: As the spectator makes their choices, secretly input them using the same SWIPE LEFT method as the digital mode.
  4. The Climax: After the third and final swipe, the app will update the database. The article on the spectator's phone will instantly change to reflect their choices. The final screen on your phone will show an image, not a QR code, which can be used for misdirection.

System Controls & Features

  • Open Settings: Tap the top-right corner of the screen.
  • Reset App: To reset for a new performance, perform a quick triple-tap on the final screen (either the QR code or the image). In Printed QR mode, this also clears the data in the database for your ID.
  • View Input History: To confirm your inputs, press and hold your finger on the final screen. An overlay showing your registered choices will appear and will vanish when you lift your finger.

Operational Tips

  • Lock Screen Rotation: Before performing, ensure your phone's screen rotation is locked to prevent layout shifts.
  • Natural Handling: Hold the phone naturally. Using your thumb to perform the swipes often looks the most casual.
  • Practice: Rehearse the gesture sequences until they become automatic. Your actions should appear as if you are simply sliding through photos.
